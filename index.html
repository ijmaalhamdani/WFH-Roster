Work From Home Policy

O3 Interfaces  ·  Design Team  ·  Refer to full HR policy for complete provisions

1. Purpose

  • This policy establishes a standardised framework governing WFH arrangements, ensuring operational efficiency, accountability, and business continuity while enabling controlled flexibility for employees.
  • WFH is a privilege extended by the organisation — not a contractual entitlement — and is contingent upon business need, individual performance, role suitability, and full adherence to this policy.

2. Scope & Eligibility

  • Applies to all permanent, full-time employees whose roles are operationally suitable for remote work.
  • Employees on probation, interns, trainees, contractual or part-time staff are not eligible unless explicitly approved via the Exception Approval Process.
  • Employees on an active PIP, subject to disciplinary proceedings, or under a termination notice are excluded.
  • Eligible employees must have completed probation, achieved a minimum "Meets Expectations" rating, and submitted a Home Workspace Self-Certification Form to HR.

3. Entitlement & Scheduling

  • Each eligible employee is entitled to up to 2 WFH days per week.
  • Monthly WFH rosters must be submitted to HR by the 25th of the preceding month, approved by the Department Head, and distributed to all team members in writing.
  • WFH days are non-transferable — employees may not swap or exchange days.
  • Managers are responsible for ensuring adequate onsite coverage and operational continuity at all times.

4. Business Requirement Overrides

  • Employees may be required to report onsite on a designated WFH day due to business exigencies. A minimum of 24 hours' notice must be given except in genuine emergencies.
  • Overrides are capped at 2 per employee per calendar month and must be logged by the Manager in the HR system prior to activation.
  • WFH days impacted by overrides will not be compensated, rescheduled, or carried forward.

5. Availability & Conduct

  • Employees must adhere to official working hours and remain fully available throughout.
  • All official communications must be responded to within 10 minutes on LarkSuite during working hours. Employees must be able to join an ad hoc call within 5 minutes of being contacted.
  • Attendance at all scheduled meetings is mandatory. Camera must remain on during virtual meetings. Camera-off exceptions require written approval from the reporting Manager and must be logged in the HR system.
  • Attendance must be marked daily via PayPeople. Daily deliverables must be logged on LarkSuite at the start and close of each WFH day.
  • If unable to work on a scheduled WFH day, the employee must apply for leave — WFH is not a substitute for leave.

6. Data Security

  • Company data must not be accessed over unsecured or public Wi-Fi without an approved VPN connection.
  • Personal devices may only be used where explicitly authorised by IT.
  • All confidentiality and NDA obligations remain fully in force during remote work.
  • Any data security incident during WFH must be reported to IT and HR immediately.

7. Dot-Based Accountability

  • Each employee is allocated 3 dots per calendar year (January–December). Zero dots is the expected outcome for a compliant employee.
  • Dot 1 — Formal written warning + 30-day enhanced monitoring period.
  • Dot 2 — WFH suspended for 30 calendar days. Reinstatement subject to satisfactory performance assessment.
  • Dot 3 — Full revocation of WFH for the remainder of the calendar year.
  • No dot may be issued verbally or retroactively. The employee has 3 working days to submit a written response to any dot notice. HR issues a final decision within 5 working days.
  • Dots expire on 31 December each year and do not carry forward.
  • Grounds for dot allocation include: missed deadlines without valid justification, unattended meetings, delayed responsiveness, and consistent underperformance.

8. Performance

  • WFH is strictly performance-driven. Employees must maintain productivity, quality, and adherence to timelines.
  • A performance decline is defined as 2+ missed committed deadlines in any rolling 4-week WFH period, KPI scores below minimum threshold for 2 consecutive WFH weeks, or a formal written performance concern raised by the Manager.
  • Corrective action follows a progressive sequence: written warning → temporary WFH suspension → Performance Improvement Plan.

9. Force Majeure & Outages

  • If rendered unavailable due to load-shedding or network outage, the employee must notify their Manager within 15 minutes via any available channel.
  • If the disruption exceeds 2 continuous hours, the employee must report onsite or apply for leave.
  • Outages reported and logged in good faith will not attract a dot allocation.

10. Non-Compliance

  • Non-compliance may result in disciplinary action, including revocation of WFH privileges.
  • Serious incidents — including misrepresentation of work status, falsification of output records, or a data security breach — may be referred directly to the formal disciplinary process, bypassing the dot ladder.
  • This policy is reviewed annually. Any material amendment will be communicated in writing a minimum of 14 calendar days before the effective date.
